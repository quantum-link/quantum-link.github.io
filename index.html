Financial Research

Our research spans the intersection of collective human dynamics, quantum graph theory, and intelligent agent systems. We aim to understand and model the behavior of modern financial markets as complex, self-organizing networks.

1. Social Dynamics Complex (SDC)

Inspired by brain connectome harmonics, the SDC models global human behavior as a "Social Connectome". We analyze multi-platform activity across sectors to extract opinion dynamics, emotion flows, and market influence signals.

  • Social Media: YouTube, X (Twitter), TikTok, LinkedIn
  • E-Commerce: Amazon, eBay, Walmart, Shopify
  • Search Trends: Google Trends, Wikipedia, Bing
  • News Sentiment: NY Times, BBC, The Guardian
  • Market Data: Yahoo Finance, Finnhub, CoinMarketCap
  • Mobility Signals: Google Maps, Uber, Lyft
  • Gaming Activity: Twitch, Steam, Roblox
  • Health Patterns: Apple HealthKit, WebMD

2. Financial Connectome (Finatom) & Quantum Signals

Our Finatom framework models the S&P 500 and global markets as evolving graph systems. Nodes represent assets and indicators; edges reflect dynamic causal and statistical relationships.

From this, we derive quantum-classical indicators such as:

  • Market Synchronization Index (MSI)
  • Eigenvalue Spread (EVS)
  • Phase Coherence Indicator (PCI)
  • Dominant Mode Oscillator (DMO)
  • Connectome Volatility Index (CVI)
  • Normal Mode Moving Averages (NMMA)

3. Agentome Framework

The Agentome is a cognitive multi-agent AI system that mimics the modular architecture of decision-making in biological systems. Agents specialize in perception, reasoning, memory, and action ‚Äî synchronized via the SPX Protocol.

  • Perception Agents: Sentiment, indicators, event readers
  • Cognitive Agents: Pattern inference, signal ranking
  • Execution Agents: Trade execution, risk calibration
  • Feedback Agents: Reward shaping and memory updates

4. MARL: Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning

Our MARL engine evolves agent policies using real-time feedback from MST indicators, sentiment, and reward functions. It is entropy-aware, context-adaptive, and supports continuous learning.

  • Policy evolution via market-aware rewards
  • Multi-agent cooperation and competition
  • Cross-agent memory and strategic refinement
